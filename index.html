About Me

Hi, my name is Joel Garcia-Cervantes. I'm a computer science student at Portland State University, on track to complete my bachelor's and begin my master's in the same field. I come from a Hispanic background and I'm a first-generation college student from an immigrant family. My academic and professional interests lie at the intersection of software engineering and artificial intelligence, where I'm actively exploring how to integrate tools like LangChain, LlamaIndex, and neural networks into real-world applications. I'm especially passionate about learning what AI is capable of to build solutions that solve meaningful problems and improve lives. Currently, I'm developing my skills as a full-stack developer — building dynamic, user-friendly interfaces with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and React. I also plan to expand into backend technologies.


Outside the classroom, I serve as president of PSU's SHPE chapter, where I lead initiatives to support Hispanic and underrepresented students in STEM. Together with my chapter we have been able to create a STEM community for underrepresented groups on campus! On my free time I also enjoy helping lower dvision students tackle difficult concepts and solidify my own knowledge about them. Some of my other hobbies include futbol, the gym, and finding good food spots.

Technical Skills

  • full-stack web development

    I'm actively developing my skills in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and React to build dynamic, user-facing interfaces for web applications. At the same time, I'm expanding into backend technologies to become a well-rounded full-stack developer capable of building complete, end-to-end applications. This foundation is essential for my goal of integrating AI into real-world software—allowing me to handle everything from the user interface to the server-side logic needed to support intelligent features.

  • applied ai and machine learning tools

    I take a hands on approach to learn applied AI tools like YOLOv8 for computer vision, and LangChain and LlamaIndex for building intelligent agents that reason over data and interact with users. I'm focused on integrating these frameworks into real-world applications that mirror how modern AI systems operate. Whether it’s experimenting with object detection pipelines or building context-aware agents, I’m learning how to harness these tools to solve meaningful problems. This hands-on approach directly supports my goal of developing next-generation applications that don’t just run—but understand, adapt, and respond intelligently.

  • data structures and algorithms

    I built a custom language interpreter using the Lark parsing library, applying recursive tree structures, expression evaluation, and memory management—strengthening my ability to think algorithmically. I’ve also studied machine learning algorithms related to Gaussian filtering and computer vision as part of my AI coursework, deepening my understanding of how foundational algorithms drive intelligent systems. Beyond my own work, I’ve consistently helped classmates grasp recursion, linked lists, and dynamic programming, which has reinforced my own skills and sharpened my ability to break down complex topics—critical for collaborative development of AI-integrated applications.

  • technical communication and leadership

    Through my role as president of PSU’s SHPE chapter, I’ve developed strong technical communication and leadership skills by organizing workshops, leading team initiatives, and mentoring peers in STEM. I’m continually working to bridge the gap between complex technical concepts and accessible explanations—whether guiding students through algorithms or collaborating with fellow officers. These experiences have taught me how to lead with clarity, listen actively, and communicate technical ideas across diverse teams.
